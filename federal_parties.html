üî¥ Liberal Party of Canada (LPC)

Founded: 1867

Leader: Justin Trudeau

Ideology: Centre to Centre-Left

The Liberal Party supports progressive policies, multiculturalism, and government intervention in the economy. It advocates for universal healthcare, environmental protections, and economic policies that blend market competition with social safety nets.

Key Policies:

  • Carbon pricing and climate action
  • Affordable childcare program
  • Tax cuts for the middle class
  • Strengthened gun control laws

üîµ Conservative Party of Canada (CPC)

Founded: 2003 (merger of PC & Reform Party)

Leader: Pierre Poilievre

Ideology: Centre-Right to Right

The Conservatives emphasize lower taxes, free-market policies, and limited government intervention. They advocate for strong national defense, reduced corporate regulations, and balancing the budget.

Key Policies:

  • Tax cuts for businesses and individuals
  • Pipeline expansion and energy independence
  • Increased funding for law enforcement
  • Opposition to carbon tax

üü† New Democratic Party (NDP)

Founded: 1961

Leader: Jagmeet Singh

Ideology: Left-Wing, Democratic Socialism

The NDP prioritizes workers' rights, wealth redistribution, and public services. It supports increasing corporate taxes, expanding universal healthcare, and improving social welfare programs.

Key Policies:

  • Universal pharmacare and dental care
  • Higher taxes on the wealthy
  • Strengthened labor rights
  • Increased investment in affordable housing

üü¢ Green Party of Canada

Founded: 1983

Leader: Elizabeth May

Ideology: Environmentalism, Progressive

The Green Party focuses on climate change action, sustainable development, and grassroots democracy. It promotes renewable energy, cutting fossil fuel subsidies, and enhancing public transportation.

Key Policies:

  • Net-zero carbon emissions by 2050
  • Ban on fracking and new oil projects
  • Guaranteed livable income
  • Electoral reform (proportional representation)
